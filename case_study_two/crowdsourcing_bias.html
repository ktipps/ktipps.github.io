Crowdsourcing a Solution to Algorithmic Bias on Facebook


Qualitative User Research Lead

Duration: 5 Months

Team: Kara Tippins Dor, Claire Lee, Sarah Chen, and Sally Chen



How might we educate & empower users to report algorithmic bias on Facebook?


The Problem

Algorithms carry an inherent risk. They aren’t created in a vacuum, and the developers making them or the data sets leveraged for training these models can bring intermixed bias.

At its worst, this bias can be racist or discriminate against specific gender or sexual identities, making the difference in how a teenager perceives themselves or whether a woman gets hired.

The Solution

Given the stakes, our team wanted to figure out how to educate & empower users to report the bias they encounter in order to make Facebook a safer place.

That's why we created Facebook Bias Bot. Facebook bias bot is a chat feature where users can pose questions about algorithmic bias they encounter and receive clear feedback.

Research Methods

Research Timeline

Targeting Active Social Media Users

We choose to focus on active Facebook users who frequently use the platform, but are also active on other social media sites as well, in order to get a sense of people's opinions on other platforms' approaches to algorithmic bias.

Key Insights from the Research

Increased Feedback on Reports

Our research showed no one wanted to wonder where their report went and what will be done with it. Users wanted feedback on the impact on their report to avoid feeling like a 'drop in the bucket.'

Clear Definition of Algorithmic Bias

Most people I spoke to had trouble actually defining what constituted algorithmic bias or giving examples. This demonstrated a need to give clear definitions of what algorithmic bias is in practice.



Motivating Users to Take Action

People felt motivated to report algorithmic bias only when they are personally offended or a community they care about is impacted.



Feature Discoverability

If the feature wasn't easy to discover, user interest dramatically declined. Users enjoy being ingrained in the flow of their social media consumption and don't want to disrupt their experience with having to exit the platform.

Meet Facebook Bias Bot




As our research showed, most people need clear definitions of what consistutes algorithmic bias.

This feature would allow users to pose scenarios they face in a safe environment, and the bot would use predetermined guidelines and question series to answer their questions. It could also recommend helpful articles based on keywords in the user's responses.

We choose an automatic bot because customer service reps would be immensely expensive for a platform with a large user base like Facebook, which also has time zone differences for its users.

Final Thoughts & Implications

We imagine Facebook Bias Bot would be one feature in a social media environment on Facebook. In this environment, we imagine users could track a ticket about their report, see its status, and receive opt-in, sporadic updates about how many people reported similar content.

Due to the project requirements, we were not able to enter the prototyping and evaluative research phases of this project. If given more time, I would have loved to prototype and further test these concepts.

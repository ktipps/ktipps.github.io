Reducing Self-Consciousness in Remote Classroms


User Research Lead & Project Manager

Duration: 5 Months



How might we reduce graduate students' self-consciousness in remote classrooms?



When COVID-19 hit in 2019, students and teachers around the world were forced to abondon their classrooms in favor of remote learning environments like Zoom.

The Problem

Video conferencing platforms assist students’ learning, but they also leave some people feeling self-conscious about their appearance, background, and general performance in a recorded environment. These factors can distract students and negatively impact their learning.

The Solution

A design intervention that leverages digital avatars and self-affirming prompts to reduce physical judgements about apperance, bolster graduate students’ self-esteem and confidence, and preserve important body language cues.

Research Methods

Research Timeline

Persuasive Pyschological Methods Used

Priming & Self-affirmation

By requiring students to input their core personal traits when onboarding, this intervention self-affirms students' core values about themselves.

Studies have shown this method bolsters students' confidence. Linking emojis to these values and having those emojis reappear throughout the user's experience on Zoom would only reinforce users' values through priming.

Pyschological Distancing

Our literature review and focus group indicated that concerns about appearance is a major cause of self-consciousness on Zoom.

To help alleviate this concern, we wanted to apply avatar filters, which pyschologically distance users from their own appearance while still giving body language feedback to everyone around them.

The Solution: Affirmed Avatars

This study allowed me to led recruitment for the city of Houston, and experience live testing with a Wizard of Oz protocol. The results of our study should be released in the coming months.

Next Steps

In our final round of testing users felt that some of the integretions of the emojis did not remind them of their value traits. As a next step, we would like to examine the impact the intervention may have post-experiment surveys with attitudal scales in larger sample size experiments.

Investigating the Pyschological Impacts of Racial Disparities in VUIs


User Research Assistant

Duration: 4 Months


The Challenge

Voice User Interfaces are extremely popular from Amazon Alexa to Google Home, but as studies have shown these technologies often showcase disparities in recognition depending on a user's background, race, or accent.


The Research Goal

Our team sought to investigate the potential pyschological consequences of repeated failed recognition attempts by a VUI system for white individuals and black individuals in five major American cities.

Methodology

Over the course of five months, our research team conducted nearly 40 user experiment sessions using a Wizard of Oz methodogy combined with a post-experiment survey to measure the pyschological impact on users' when a VUI system failed to recognize their voice.

Learning to Wizard of Oz

Working the behind-the-scenes mechanisms was a big lesson for me! We leveraged a combination of Keynote and an audio playout software named Play.HT to create the effect that someone was speaking to a real VUI. As a research assitant, I was responsible for making sure that the timing of the playout matched the screen that the user saw, which could be difficult as you're managing participants.

Dealing with Scams in Recruitment

One challenge our team faced in recruitment was scammers, who motivated by the financial incentive mentioned in our Craiglist posting, would lie about their race or location. We had a couple of people attend the study, and were clearly not who they identified themselves to be on the screener. In response, we revised our screener to be more specific and altered our protocol to conclude the interview when the researcher recognized someone lied.

Next Steps

This study allowed me to led recruitment for the city of Houston, and experience live testing with a Wizard of Oz protocol. The results of our study should be released in the coming months.
